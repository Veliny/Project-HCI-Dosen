What is Climate Change?
To understand Climate Change first we must understand what a climate is and how is and it's differences from weather.

Simply the Climate is state and conditions of the atmosphere or sky on a large time-scale or a long period of time for a particular area or region.
While the weather is the state and conditions of the atmosphere or sky on a small period of time, usually weather indicate what the changes in the atmosphere in the scale of minutes to months.

Knowing what a climate is and it's differeces with weather , know the term climate change becomes clearer. Climate Change is the changing of the usual patterns and conditions of the atmosphere.
This effects the normal weather patterns, resulting in the change in frequency of certain weathers, for example getting less snow or the opposite, getting more snow at a certain time in the year.

Is the Climate Changing?
Yes the climate is changing.
The recent trend of the climate is that the avarage tempature of the earth is slowly getting higher/warmer. This has cause significant effects such as rising sea levels and warmer oceans.

What is Causing the Change?
There has been many fluctuations in the climate in the past thousands to millions of years ago where the climate is warmer or colder. Thousands of years ago where human have little ways to effect the climate or when humans didn't exists yet the climate can change through natural causes , distance from the sun, the state of the sun can change the amount of energy it radiates to earth, natural disasters like volcanic erruption can spew volcanic ash enough to block the sun the cool the earth.

Apart from the natural causes and factors from nature, humans obviously can also effect the climate. Our actions that consumes energy inderectly can change the climate because of most of those energy we consume comes from burning coal, oil and gasses that produces gasses like CO2 that can warm the atmosphere.
